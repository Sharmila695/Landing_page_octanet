About Vishakapatnam

Visakhapatnam, also known as Vizag, is the largest and most populous metropolitan city in the Indian state of Andhra Pradesh. It’s a major commercial and administrative center with road, rail, and air connections.Located between the Eastern Ghats and the coast of the Bay of Bengal, it’s known for its unspoiled sandy beaches, well-maintained parks, and nearby scenic areas like the Araku Valley. The city has a rich history dating back to the 6th century BCE. Today, it’s a significant contributor to India’s GDP and is one of the four smart cities selected under the Smart Cities Mission

Some Cool Stories

Maritime Marvels: A Port City Par Excellence: Visakhapatnam Port is the fifth-busiest port in all of India. It handles cargo like coal, iron ore, petroleum products, and containers. Bheemunipatnam Beach, named after Bheema Sena from the Indian epic Mahabharata, was part of an old Dutch settlement. The ruins have been preserved and serve as a reminder of our colonial past

Cultural Heritage: Temples and Festivals1: The Simhachalam Temple, one of the most revered Hindu temples in Visakhapatnam, is dedicated to Lord Varaha Narasimha. The exact construction date of this temple is unknown. The city also hosts the Visakha Utsav, a festival featuring traditional music and dance performances, food stalls, art exhibitions, and a colorful parade.

Industrial Hub: Driving Economic Growth: Visakhapatnam houses five steel plants within a 20km radius. These plants play a key role in steel production and have opened many doors for ancillary industries. The Visakhapatnam Special Economic Zone (VSEZ) attracts foreign investments and houses a wide range of industries.
